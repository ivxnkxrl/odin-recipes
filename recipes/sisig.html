Sisig Recipe

Sisig is a beloved Filipino dish originating from Pampanga, Philippines.

Sisig is made of chopped pork (traditionally pig face and belly) seasoned with calamansi, soy sauce, and chilies.

It is typically mixed with chicken liver and mayonnaise for creaminess, then served piping hot on a sizzling metal plate and topped with a raw egg.

Ingredients

Main:

  • 1 lb of Pork Belly
  • 2 Onions
  • 4 thumbs of Ginger
  • 2 tablespoon of Salt
  • 3 pieces of Bay Leaves
  • Water

Dressing:

  • 1/2 cup of Mayonnaise
  • 2 tablespoon of Vinegar
  • 1/4 teaspoon of Ground Black Pepper
  • 1 teaspoon of Salt
  • 1 teaspoon of Sugar
  • 1/4 cup of Liver Spread
  • 2 Limes

Steps

  1. Combine pork belly, ginger, bay leaves, 2 teaspoons salt, and water in a cooking pot. Boil for 1 hour. Remove from the pot and drain the water.
  2. Heat-up your grill. Start grilling the pork belly for around 5 minutes per side. Remove from the grill afterwards and let it cool down.
  3. Make the dressing by combining all the dressing ingredients. Mix well.
  4. Chop the grilled pork belly into small pieces. In a large mixing bowl, combine the chopped pork with onion and the dressing mixture. Toss until all the ingredients are well blended.
  5. Transfer to a serving plate. Serve with your favorite drink. Share and enjoy!
